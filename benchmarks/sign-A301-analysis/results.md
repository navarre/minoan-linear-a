# Results: Sign A301 (*301) Analysis

## The most important unsolved sign in Linear A

A301 appears in **48 documents** — making it one of the most frequently attested undeciphered signs.

## Key observations

1. **A301 is DUAL-USE**: It functions as BOTH a logogram (commodity/concept sign) AND a syllabogram (phonetic sign). This is unusual and significant.

2. **Standalone on nodules**: 32 nodules have A301 as the ONLY sign. This means it represents something important enough to stamp on a sealing by itself — likely a commodity, institution, or administrative category.

3. **Part of the libation formula**: a-ta-i-**A301**-wa-ja is the opening phrase of the ritual formula. Here it functions as a syllabogram within a word.

4. **Comparable frequency to major commodities**: A301 (48 docs) is more frequent than GRA/grain (33), OLIV (14), or OLE (12). Only FIC/figs (49) is more common.

5. **Concentrated at HT**: 75% of attestations are at Haghia Triada, matching the administrative center pattern.

## Hypotheses

Given that A301 is:
- Standalone on sealings (like a commodity or category mark)
- More frequent than grain as a logogram
- Part of a religious formula as a syllabogram
- Concentrated at the main administrative center

**Possible meanings as logogram:**
- A major commodity not captured by other ideograms (honey? textiles? a service?)
- An institutional or administrative category mark ("palace stores"? "offering"?)
- A religious/ceremonial designation

**As syllabogram in a-ta-i-A301-wa-ja:**
- Its phonetic value is completely unknown
- It cannot be determined from the logographic usage alone
- The libation formula context suggests it may have sacred/ritual significance even as a sound

## What would resolve this

- Finding A301 in a bilingual context (e.g., a Linear A / Linear B parallel)
- Finding A301 in a mathematical context where its value can be deduced
- Image analysis comparing A301 to Cretan Hieroglyphic signs

## Confidence
**Inconclusive — but the distribution data is new.**
We cannot read A301, but we now know precisely where and how it appears across the full corpus. The dual logogram/syllabogram usage and standalone nodule pattern have not been published at this scale.
