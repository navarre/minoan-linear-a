# Results: Compound Words and Word Families

## Corpus summary
- Unique words: **1,025**
- Multi-syllable words: **951**
- Source: Full imported SigLA corpus

## Compound words

21 words contain `ku-ro` ("total") as a component, confirming it functions as a productive morpheme:
- `po-to-ku-ro` (3x) — "grand total" (po-to + ku-ro)
- `ku-ro-sa-ra` (1x) — ku-ro + sa-ra (total + administrative term)
- `ne-ku-ro-ku-ro` (1x) — reduplicated ku-ro
- `mu-ru-ku-ro` (1x) — mu-ru + ku-ro
- Plus 17 longer words ending in ku-ro (tablet-length entries)

6 words contain `ki-ro` ("deficit") as a component, parallel to ku-ro compounds.

## Key findings

1. **ku-ro is a productive suffix**, not just a standalone word — it attaches to other morphemes to form compound totals
2. **The a- prefix is the most productive morpheme** in the language (8 of 10 largest word families)
3. **-ta-na is a recurring suffix** (6 words), possibly a place-name or ethnic suffix (cf. ki-re-ta-na)
4. **-mi-na is a recurring suffix** (6 words), possibly nominal (cf. ku-mi-na, a-ku-mi-na)
5. **Word families show systematic derivation**: a-du → a-du-re, a-du-za, a-du-ni-ta-na (same root, different suffixes)

## Interpretation

The word family analysis reveals Linear A as a **productive morphological system** with:
- A dominant prefix (a-)
- Multiple productive suffixes (-ku-ro, -ki-ro, -ta-na, -mi-na, -sa-ra)
- Compound word formation (po-to + ku-ro, ku-ro + sa-ra)
- Systematic derivation from roots

This is consistent with an **agglutinative or moderately synthetic language** — not isolating (like Chinese) or heavily fusional (like Latin).

## Confidence
**Provisional success.**
Based on 1,025 unique words from the full corpus. The a- prefix dominance and ku-ro compounding are robust findings. Some "families" may be coincidental string matches rather than true morphological relationships, especially for short roots.
