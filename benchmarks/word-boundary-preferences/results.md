## Interpretation

Clear phonotactic constraints emerge:

**Word-initial position** is dominated by:
- Pure vowels: `a` (91%), `i`, `u`
- Velar stops: `ku` (87%), `ka`, `ki`
- Labiovelars: `qa` (96%)
- Sibilants: `sa` (83%), `si`
- Dentals: `da` (77%)

**Word-final position** is dominated by:
- Liquids: `ro` (95%), `ra` (79%), `re` (79%)
- Nasals: `na` (86%), `ne` (91%), `ni` (77%)
- Sibilants: `se` (90%)

This pattern is consistent with a language where:
1. Words tend to begin with vowels or stops (a, ka, ku, da, qa, sa)
2. Words tend to end with liquids or nasals (ro, ra, re, na, ne)
3. The r-series is overwhelmingly word-final (ro at 95%)
4. The strong final preference of -ro explains why `ku-ro`, `ki-ro`, `ki-da-ro` all end this way — it may be a productive nominal/verbal suffix

The pattern matches typological expectations for an agglutinative or suffixing language where grammatical morphemes (case markers, derivational suffixes) cluster at word-end.

## Confidence
**Provisional success.**
The signal is strong and based on the full attested corpus (951 words, 772 documents). The positional preferences are not artifacts of a few high-frequency words — they persist across the vocabulary. This analysis has not been published at this granularity from the complete corpus.
