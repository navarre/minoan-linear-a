# Re-discovery of the libation formula family from ritual corpus structure

## Summary
This benchmark tests whether the current corpus can recover a recurring libation-formula family from ritual texts without beginning from the known wording as the answer.

## Why it matters
This is a **replication benchmark**. It checks whether the corpus can recover not just administrative markers, but also a repeated ritual formula family with a stable core and variable extensions.

## Headline result
A semi-blind structural analysis of ritual texts re-discovers a strongly ritual-exclusive formula family centered on `a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja`, most commonly extended by `ja-sa-sa-ra-me`, and less often by `i-da-ma-te`.

## Status
**provisional success**

## Related findings
- `a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja` appears in 21 ritual documents and 0 non-ritual documents.
- `a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja . ja-sa-sa-ra-me` is the strongest repeated ritual bigram.
- `a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja . ja-sa-sa-ra-me . i-da-ma-te` recurs as a longer extension tier.
