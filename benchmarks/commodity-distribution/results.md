## Previously unknown findings

1. **FIC (figs) is the most common commodity** — appearing on 49 documents, more than grain (33) or wine (39). This was not apparent from the Python subset (which had grain as dominant). Figs may have been a more important staple than previously emphasized.

2. **Unknown sign A301 appears on 33 documents** — as frequently as grain. This is the most important undeciphered sign in the corpus (also appears in the libation formula). Its frequency as a logogram suggests it represents a major commodity or concept.

3. **Fraction signs are abundant** (313 occurrences) — Linear A's unique fraction system appears on a significant portion of documents, suggesting fractional accounting was routine.

4. **Transaction signs** (35 definite + 26 probable = 61 total) — These specialized signs mark transaction types and have no Linear B parallel.

## Interpretation

The commodity distribution reveals a palatial economy centered on agricultural products (figs, grain, olives), liquids (wine, oil), and livestock (sheep). The prominence of figs is a potential novel finding that differs from the grain-centric view derived from smaller corpus samples.
