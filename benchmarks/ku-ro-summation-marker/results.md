# Results

## Corpus summary
- Total inscriptions analyzed: **127**
- Quantity-bearing tablet lines detected: **289**

## Top first tokens in quantity-bearing lines
- `ku-ro` — **74**
- `a-du` — 14
- `ki-re-ta2` — 13
- `pa-ja-re` — 12
- others trail behind

`ku-ro` is the dominant first token in quantity-bearing administrative lines by a large margin. No other token comes close in the same slot.

## Example lines
- `HT 1  ku-ro . GRA 33`
- `HT 2  ku-ro . OLE+KI 20`
- `HT 6  ku-ro . GRA 13 . OLE 1 . VIN 2`
- `HT 7  ku-ro . VIN 33`
- `HT 8  ku-ro . GRA 20 . OLIV 10`
- `HT 16 ku-ro . OVIS 10`
- `HT 17 ku-ro . FIC 60`

These examples show `ku-ro` in a stable administrative position before commodity + number expressions.

## `po-to-ku-ro`
The benchmark also found a rarer extended parallel form:
- `HT 93 po-to-ku-ro . GRA 576`
- `HT 99 po-to-ku-ro . GRA 60`

This is consistent with `po-to-ku-ro` as a structurally related extended summation expression.

## `ki-ro`
Lower-frequency related administrative lines were also found:
- `HT 31 ki-ro . GRA 5`
- `HT 96 ki-ro . VIN 10`
- `ZA 10 ki-ro . OVIS 5`
- `PH 31 ki-ro . GRA 5`

## Interpretation
The corpus strongly supports `ku-ro` as the most likely summation marker in the current administrative dataset.

## Confidence
**Provisional success.**
The result is strong enough to count as an early replication benchmark, but still depends on heuristic quantity-line detection and a corpus that remains incomplete.
