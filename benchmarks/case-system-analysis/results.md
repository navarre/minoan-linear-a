## Evidence for each function

### -ro (totals/results)
- ku-ro "total", ki-ro "deficit", po-to-ku-ro "grand total"
- The three most securely read words all end in -ro
- 95% word-final = highly constrained positional distribution
- Almost exclusively on administrative tablets (96%)

### -ra (agents/categories)
- sa-ra (20x, admin term), i-ra (16x, nodule name), pu-ra, da-qe-ra
- More varied distribution than -ro (tablets + nodules)
- May mark agent or recipient in transactions

### -te (dative/locative)
- i-da-ma-te "Mother of Ida" (locative: "of Ida"?)
- a-di-ki-te "at Dikte" (locative)
- mi-nu-te, ma-ka-ri-te, ta-na-te
- 100% tablet context, 72% with ideograms
- Parallels in -te locative/dative across Mediterranean languages

### -ja (adjectival/ethnic)
- se-to-i-ja "Siteian" (ethnic of Siteia)
- pa-se-ja, su-ki-ri-te-i-ja
- Direct parallel to Linear B -ja ethnic/adjectival suffix
- Strongest cross-script evidence for shared morphology

### -me (possessive)
- ja-sa-sa-ra-me "my lady" (ishassara + -me)
- da-me "my assessment?"
- Low frequency but appears in both ritual and admin contexts
- The only suffix clearly attested in ritual texts

### -na (genitive/agentive)
- ki-re-ta-na (4x), ka-na (3x), *56-ni-na (3x)
- Often marks what appears to be personal names or agents
- May be genitive ("of X") or agentive ("the one from X")

## Summary

Linear A shows a **6-8 case system** with clear functional differentiation:
- **Core cases**: -ro (result/total), -te (locative/dative), -ja (adjectival)
- **Probable cases**: -ra (agentive), -na (genitive), -se (instrumental?)
- **Uncertain**: -ne (dative plural?), -me (possessive)

This is consistent with an agglutinative language with transparent case morphology — suffixes attach productively to roots without significant stem alternation.

## Confidence
**Provisional success.**
The distributional patterns are clear. The functional labels are interpretations that need validation against more text types and cross-linguistic parallels.
