# Linear A Decipherment Project: Success Criteria

## What does "success" look like?

This is a tiered system. Each level builds on the previous. We don't need to
"solve" Linear A in one shot - we need to make measurable, verifiable progress
that moves the field forward.

---

## Level 1: REPLICATION (Prove the tools work)
**Goal:** Independently rediscover from raw data what other researchers have already found.

- [ ] Confirm ku-ro = "total" from positional/numerical analysis alone
- [ ] Confirm ki-ro = "deficit/owed" from context patterns
- [ ] Confirm po-to-ku-ro = "grand total" from composite analysis
- [ ] Identify the libation formula (a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja ... ja-sa-sa-ra-me) as a recurring ritual phrase without being told
- [ ] Detect that HT tablets are administrative/accounting documents from structure alone
- [ ] Reproduce known sign frequency distributions matching published scholarship
- [ ] Identify word-initial and word-final sign preferences matching Younger's grammar

**Why this matters:** If our tools can't find what humans already found, they're broken.

---

## Level 2: NOVEL DISCOVERY (Find something new)
**Goal:** Discover at least one pattern, relationship, or reading that hasn't been published.

Candidates:
- [ ] A previously unnoticed morphological pattern (affix, alternation, paradigm)
- [ ] A new sign co-occurrence constraint that reveals phonotactic rules
- [ ] A statistical anomaly in a specific site's corpus suggesting dialect variation
- [ ] An unrecognized compound word or word family
- [ ] A new positional pattern distinguishing word classes (nouns vs. verbs vs. names)
- [ ] A previously unnoticed numerical relationship in the accounting tablets
- [ ] A sign sequence that appears to be a loanword into Linear B vocabulary

**Bar:** At least ONE of these, with statistical evidence strong enough to publish.

---

## Level 3: CROSS-PAPER SYNTHESIS (Connect the dots)
**Goal:** Find a meaningful connection between two or more published papers/approaches that their authors missed.

Candidates:
- [ ] Link Adithyan Madhu's Anatolian phonetic mappings with Nepal/Cacciafoco's cryptanalysis results
- [ ] Connect SigLA palaeographic variation data with Younger's grammatical observations
- [ ] Synthesize the Semitic loanword hypothesis (Gordon) with Anatolian substrate theory
- [ ] Show that frequency analysis supports (or refutes) the agglutinative typology claim
- [ ] Combine Packard's computer-verified sign equivalences with modern entropy analysis
- [ ] Link pre-Greek substrate vocabulary (-nth-, -ss- words) with specific Linear A sequences

**Bar:** A concrete, testable claim that follows logically from combining two sources.

---

## Level 4: DISPROOF (Kill a bad hypothesis)
**Goal:** Use computational evidence to definitively disprove at least one published claim.

Candidates:
- [ ] Disprove (or confirm) the Semitic hypothesis (Gordon/Best) with statistical language-distance metrics
- [ ] Disprove (or confirm) the "Minoan = language isolate" hypothesis
- [ ] Show that a specific proposed translation is statistically impossible given context
- [ ] Disprove a proposed sign value by showing it creates impossible phonotactic sequences
- [ ] Show that a proposed grammar rule fails when tested against the full corpus
- [ ] Demonstrate that a claimed "cognate" is statistically no better than chance

**Bar:** P-value < 0.05 or equivalent Bayesian evidence. Not vibes - math.

---

## Level 5: TRANSLATION (Produce a likely reading)
**Goal:** Create at least one sentence, phrase, or complete thought with very high confidence.

Priority targets (highest chance of success):
- [ ] Full translation of the standard libation formula with confidence scores per word
- [ ] Reading of a complete short accounting entry (name + commodity + number + total)
- [ ] Translation of a complete roundel inscription
- [ ] Identify at least 3 divine names with probable meanings
- [ ] Produce a glossary of 20+ words with >70% confidence ratings
- [ ] Translate one full tablet (even a short one) end-to-end

**Bar:** Each word must have:
  1. A proposed reading
  2. Evidence from at least 2 independent methods (context, morphology, cross-linguistic, frequency)
  3. An explicit confidence score
  4. Falsifiability criteria (what would prove it wrong?)

---

## Level 6: SCALE (10-25% of the project)
**Goal:** Systematic progress across the full corpus.

- [ ] All ~1,400 inscriptions digitized and searchable
- [ ] Complete sign frequency tables for all sites
- [ ] Morphological analysis of all attested words (500+ unique sequences)
- [ ] Language family hypothesis scored with Bayesian framework
- [ ] 50+ words with proposed readings (any confidence level)
- [ ] 20+ words with high-confidence readings (>70%)
- [ ] Full grammar sketch: word order, case system, verb morphology, number marking
- [ ] At least 5 complete inscription translations
- [ ] Identified the language family with >60% posterior probability
- [ ] Published-quality report with methodology, evidence, and falsifiable claims

---

## Measurement

Each level is scored independently:
- **Not started** = 0%
- **In progress** = percentage of checkboxes completed
- **Complete** = all checkboxes checked + evidence documented

Current target: **Complete Level 1 + meaningful progress on Levels 2-4** in this session.

---

## Principles

1. **Reproducibility over revelation.** Every claim must be computable from the data.
2. **Confidence scores on everything.** No unqualified assertions.
3. **The math doesn't lie.** If the statistics say the Semitic hypothesis is dead, it's dead.
4. **Fresh eyes beat old biases.** We have no reputation to protect. Test everything.
5. **One solid word is worth more than a speculative paragraph.** Depth over breadth.
6. **Show your work.** Every proposed reading must trace back to evidence.
