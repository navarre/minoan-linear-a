# The Linear A Libation Formula: Complete Analysis

## The Formula

The best-attested multi-word phrase in Linear A, found on stone offering vessels across Crete.

### Fullest Reconstruction

```
Word 1: a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja     [dedicatory opening — blocked by undeciphered *301]
Word 2: [VARIABLE: place/deity] [local sacred mountain: Dikte, Ida, Tylissos, etc.]
Word 3: [VARIABLE: personal]   [the dedicator's name]
Word 4: ja-sa-sa-ra-me         "My Lady" (Luwian ishassara + possessive -me)
Word 5: u-na-ka-na-si          "[he/she] dedicates" (agglutinative verb)
Word 6: i-pi-na-ma             "dedication / offering" (noun)
Word 7: si-ru-te               [purpose/recipient in dative — meaning unclear]
Word 8: i-da-ma-te             "Mother Ida" (i-da + PIE *mater)
```

### Tiered Structure

| Pattern | Documents |
|---------|-----------|
| Word 1 alone | ~21 |
| Word 1 + Word 4 | 11 |
| Word 1 + Word 4 + Word 8 | 4 |
| Full formula (5+ words) | 1 (KO Za 1) |

No single inscription contains all eight words.

## Key Findings

### 1. ja-sa-sa-ra-me = Luwian "My Lady"

The strongest etymological proposal in all of Linear A:
- Luwian *ishassara* ("lady") → JA-SA-SA-RA: near-perfect phonological match
- -ME = possessive "my" (independently attested in administrative texts: da-me)
- The earliest attestation at Platanos (MM II) reads A-SA-SA-RA-ME (without JA-), closer to the Luwian source
- Variant JA-SA-SA-RA-MA-NA shows -MA-NA alternating with -ME

### 2. u-na-ka-na-si = A Minoan Verb

Younger explicitly identifies this as a verb: "dedicates"
- Shows textbook agglutination: U-NA → U-NA-KA → U-NA-KA-NA → U-NA-KA-NA-SI
- Variant U-NA-RU-KA-NA-TI has -TI instead of -SI (possibly tense/number change)
- Root U-NA- has no cognate in any proposed source language — core Minoan vocabulary

### 3. i-da-ma-te = "Mother Ida"

- I-DA = Mt. Ida (pre-Greek toponym, central Crete)
- MA-TE = "mother" (PIE *meh₂tēr)
- Connects to later Rhea/Cybele mountain-mother worship
- Alternative I-NA-JA-PA-QA appears in the same slot — different deity at different sites

### 4. Sign *301: The Undeciphered Key

- **48 documents total** — more frequent than grain (33 docs)
- Dual function: **logogram** (32 cases as sole sign on nodules) AND **syllabogram** (in formula and other words)
- No Linear B equivalent → represents a non-Greek phoneme
- Manning (2009) speculatively identifies it as CHS #046 ("engraving tool") with value 'phra'
- Appears in 15+ word contexts beyond the formula (names, admin terms)
- Solving *301 could unlock the entire formula

### 5. Words Exclusively Ritual vs. Shared with Administration

| Exclusively ritual | Appears in both contexts |
|---|---|
| a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja | si-ru-te (also on HT tablets) |
| ja-sa-sa-ra-me | u-na-ka-na-si root (also on HT tablets) |
| i-da-ma-te | da-ma-te (appears independently) |

### 6. A-SA-SA-RA Predates Linear A

The sequence appears on Arkhanes seal stones (~2000 BCE), in the "Arkhanes script" that may be the ancestor of both Linear A and Cretan Hieroglyphic (Schoep 1999). The ritual vocabulary may have motivated the invention of writing on Crete.

## What the Formula Reveals About the Language

1. **Agglutinative** — u-na-ka-na-si has 4-5 stacked suffixes
2. **Not Greek, not Semitic** — morphology incompatible with both
3. **Luwian religious borrowings** — ja-sa-sa-ra-me, possibly i-da-ma-te
4. **Consistent suffix system** — same suffixes (-te, -me, -ja, -si) function identically in ritual and administrative registers
5. **Standardized across Crete** — implying centralized religious authority

## Tentative Translation

> "[Sacred formula] to [Local Deity/Mountain], [Dedicator], My Lady, dedicates this offering for [purpose], Mother Ida."
