# The Linear A Libation Formula: Complete Analysis

*Updated April 2026 with GORILA Vol 5 primary-source confirmation.*

## The Formula

The best-attested multi-word phrase in Linear A, found on stone offering vessels across Crete.

### Fullest Reconstruction

```
Word 1: a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja     [dedicatory opening — blocked by undeciphered *301]
Word 2: [VARIABLE: place/deity] [local sacred mountain: Dikte, Ida, Tylissos, etc.]
Word 3: [VARIABLE: personal]   [the dedicator's name]
Word 4: ja-sa-sa-ra-me         "My Lady" (Luwian ishassara + possessive -me)
Word 5: u-na-ka-na-si          "[he/she] dedicates" (agglutinative verb)
Word 6: i-pi-na-ma             "dedication / offering" (noun)
Word 7: si-ru-te               [purpose/recipient in dative — meaning unclear]
Word 8: i-da-ma-te             "Mother Ida" (i-da + PIE *mater)
```

### Primary-source confirmation

The canonical opening **a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja** has been confirmed at primary-source level against GORILA Vol 5 (Godart & Olivier 1985), the foundational print corpus. GORILA sign sequence: `08-59-28-301-54-57`.

Confirmed attestations on stone offering tables (Za type):

| Site | Documents |
|------|-----------|
| **Iouktas (IO)** | IO Za 1a.1, 2a, 3, 4, 7 — 5 attestations (major peak sanctuary) |
| **Palaikastro (PK)** | PK Za 11a, 12a |
| **Syme Viannou (SY)** | SY Za 1, 2a, 3 — 3 attestations |
| **Kophinas (KO)** | KO Za 1a |
| **Troullos (TL)** | TL Za 1a |
| **Apodoulou (AP)** | AP Za 1 (with `ki-ki-` prefix variant) |

**At least 13 independent stone-vessel attestations across 6 sanctuary sites.** This is primary-source validation of a reading that until now the project had inherited from Younger's Lexicon as a secondary source.

### Documented variants

Only two variants of word 1 are firmly documented in the GORILA sign index:

1. **AP Za 1** adds a `ki-ki-` prefix: `ki-ki-ta-i-*301-u-ja` (GORILA sequence 67-67-59-28-301-10-57). Apodoulou's version is the clearest local adaptation.
2. **IO Za 4** shows a partial variant with a different second syllable or a broken ending: `na-ta-i-*301-wa-[unk]` or similar.

The rest of the 13+ attestations use the canonical form without deviation. The formula opening is remarkably stable across sites — the core sacred text stayed fixed while other elements of the inscription (dedicator names, local deity epithets) varied.

### Tiered Structure

| Pattern | Documents |
|---------|-----------|

No single inscription contains all eight words.


## Key Findings

### 1. ja-sa-sa-ra-me = Luwian "My Lady"

The strongest etymological proposal in all of Linear A:
- Luwian *ishassara* ("lady") → JA-SA-SA-RA: near-perfect phonological match
- -ME = possessive "my" (independently attested in administrative texts: da-me)
- The earliest attestation at Platanos (MM II) reads A-SA-SA-RA-ME (without JA-), closer to the Luwian source
- Variant JA-SA-SA-RA-MA-NA shows -MA-NA alternating with -ME

### 2. u-na-ka-na-si = A Minoan Verb

Younger explicitly identifies this as a verb: "dedicates"
- Shows textbook agglutination: U-NA → U-NA-KA → U-NA-KA-NA → U-NA-KA-NA-SI
- Variant U-NA-RU-KA-NA-TI has -TI instead of -SI (possibly tense/number change)
- Root U-NA- has no cognate in any proposed source language — core Minoan vocabulary

### 3. i-da-ma-te = "Mother Ida"

- I-DA = Mt. Ida (pre-Greek toponym, central Crete)
- MA-TE = "mother" (PIE *meh₂tēr)
- Connects to later Rhea/Cybele mountain-mother worship
- Alternative I-NA-JA-PA-QA appears in the same slot — different deity at different sites

### 4. Sign *301: The Undeciphered Key

- **48 documents total** — more frequent than grain (33 docs)
- Dual function: **logogram** (32 cases as sole sign on nodules) AND **syllabogram** (in formula and other words)
- No Linear B equivalent → represents a non-Greek phoneme
- Manning (2009) speculatively identifies it as CHS #046 ("engraving tool") with value 'phra'
- Appears in 15+ word contexts beyond the formula (names, admin terms)
- Solving *301 could unlock the entire formula

### 5. Words Exclusively Ritual vs. Shared with Administration

| Exclusively ritual | Appears in both contexts |
|---|---|
| a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja | si-ru-te (also on HT tablets) |
| ja-sa-sa-ra-me | u-na-ka-na-si root (also on HT tablets) |
| i-da-ma-te | da-ma-te (appears independently) |

### 6. A-SA-SA-RA Predates Linear A

The sequence appears on Arkhanes seal stones (~2000 BCE), in the "Arkhanes script" that may be the ancestor of both Linear A and Cretan Hieroglyphic (Schoep 1999). The ritual vocabulary may have motivated the invention of writing on Crete.

## What the Formula Reveals About the Language

1. **Agglutinative** — u-na-ka-na-si has 4-5 stacked suffixes
2. **Not Greek, not Semitic** — morphology incompatible with both
3. **Luwian religious borrowings** — ja-sa-sa-ra-me, possibly i-da-ma-te
4. **Consistent suffix system** — same suffixes (-te, -me, -ja, -si) function identically in ritual and administrative registers
5. **Standardized across Crete** — implying centralized religious authority

## Tentative Translation

> "[Sacred formula] to [Local Deity/Mountain], [Dedicator], My Lady, dedicates this offering for [purpose], Mother Ida."

### Tier-structure reconciliation

The original tier table (Word 1 alone: ~21, Word 1+4: 11, Word 1+4+8: 4, full: 1) does not match the current data layer. Audited counts against `corpus.json`:

| Pattern | Original | Audited | Documents |
|---------|:---:|:---:|---|
| Word 1 (any *301 formula opening) present | ~21 | **18** | AP Za 1, HT 98a (admin), IO Za 2, 3, 6, 7, 8, PK Za 11, 12, PS Za 2, SY Za 1, 3, 4, 8, TL Za 1, TY Zb 4, ZA Zb 3, + KO Za 1 |
| Word 1 + Word 4 (ja-sa-sa-ra-me) | 11 | **4** | IO Za 2, IO Za 6, PS Za 2, TL Za 1 |
| Word 1 + Word 4 + Word 8 (i-da-ma-te) | 4 | **0** | — i-da-ma-te never co-occurs with Word 1 in the current corpus |
| Word 1 + Word 4 + Word 5 (u-na-ka-na-si) | — | **1** | IO Za 2 |
| All three of Words 1, 4, 5, and 8 | 1 (KO Za 1) | **0** | KO Za 1 has Word 1 + Word 5 + Word 4 but not Word 8 as tokenized |

Two additional findings from the reconciliation:

- **Word 8 (i-da-ma-te / "Mother Ida") is geographically restricted.** All three attestations — `i-da-ma-te` at Arkhanes Fourni (AR Zf 1, AR Zf 2) and cognate `da-ma-te` at Kythera (KY Za 2) — appear without Word 1 in the current data layer. The theonym is not part of the libation formula as it's currently tokenized; it's a separate dedicatory phrase on its own documents. The public page should present i-da-ma-te as a *related* ritual expression, not as a member of the libation formula proper.
- **Word 4 (ja-sa-sa-ra-me) independently appears at 4 sites without Word 1** (IO Za 9, IO Zb 10, PK Za 27, PL Zf 1). This means ja-sa-sa-ra-me is an independent ritual expression that *often* co-occurs with the *301 opening but is not bound to it.

The original "~21" for Word 1 likely counted logogram-only uses of *301 (48 in the audited data) merged with running-text uses, or used an earlier tokenization. The 11/4 co-occurrence figures in the original are not reproducible from the current corpus and should be replaced. The new framing: **Word 1 and Word 4 are two independent ritual expressions that each appear on their own ≈4–5 times and co-occur 4 times** — that's the defensible public statement.
