# Verifier R7 — Luwian / Anatolian Contact Beyond the Formula

## Task
Test whether Anatolian/Luwian contact in Linear A extends beyond the libation formula, and whether it is limited to ritual language or appears in administrative vocabulary as well.

## Sources used in this pass
- `docs/analysis/libation-formula.md`
- `docs/analysis/substrate-words.md`
- `docs/analysis/hypothesis-testing.md`
- project context from prior verifier tasks, including the 21 LA-LB parallels note and the *301 / glossary work

## 1. Secure starting point
The strongest Anatolian/Luwian contact claim in the current project remains:

- **ja-sa-sa-ra-me** ← Luwian **ishassara** + possessive **-me** = “My Lady”

This is still the cleanest and most persuasive external etymology in the project.

A second often-cited candidate is:
- **du-pu₂-re** ↔ Hittite/Luwian royal-title zone around **Tabarna / Labarna**

That proposal is intriguing but less secure than `ja-sa-sa-ra-me`, because it depends on a longer chain of phonological/semantic transformation.

## 2. Candidate contact words beyond the formula
From the current analysis set, the plausible Anatolian-contact zone extends beyond `ja-sa-sa-ra-me`, but not uniformly across the corpus.

### Strongest or strongest-adjacent candidates
1. **ja-sa-sa-ra-me**  
   - ritual formula only  
   - best candidate for direct Luwian borrowing

2. **du-pu₂-re**  
   - ritual / elite title zone  
   - possible Anatolian royal-title connection  
   - plausible, but weaker than `ja-sa-sa-ra-me`

3. **i-da-ma-te**  
   - religious formula / divine-name zone  
   - not a clean Luwian borrowing in the same way, but it participates in the same ritual register where Anatolian contact is most visible

4. possible substrate-to-Greek survivals discussed in `substrate-words.md`  
   - **ku-pa-ri** → kyparissos  
   - **du-pu₂-re** → labyrinthos chain  
   - these are more about long-term substrate survival than direct Luwian contact, but they reinforce that the religious/elite lexicon is the most contact-sensitive zone

## 3. Where contact appears
### Ritual / religious register
This is where the Anatolian evidence is strongest.

Evidence:
- the libation formula is the core ritual text
- `ja-sa-sa-ra-me` is explicitly religious
- `du-pu₂-re` is discussed in relation to lordship / title / sacred-palatial authority
- `i-da-ma-te` belongs to deity / mountain / cult vocabulary

### Administrative register
Current evidence for direct Anatolian/Luwian borrowings in administration is weak.

The best-understood administrative core vocabulary remains mostly:
- **ku-ro**
- **ki-ro**
- **po-to-ku-ro**
- **da-me**
- commodity terms and headings

These are central to the corpus, and the current project analysis does **not** show them as strong Luwian borrowings.

That asymmetry matters.

## 4. Best current answer to the task question
### Is Anatolian contact limited to religious vocabulary?
**Mostly yes, on current evidence.**

The current project evidence suggests that the clearest Anatolian/Luwian signals cluster in:
- cult vocabulary
- divine epithets
- elite or sacred title language
- possibly prestige vocabulary around ritual and authority

It does **not** yet show the same level of Anatolian penetration in the most basic accounting / commodity / totals vocabulary.

## 5. What that implies
If Anatolian/Luwian contact is concentrated in the religious register, the most plausible models are:

### A. Religious prestige borrowing
Minoan ritual language borrowed specific high-status sacred terms from the Anatolian sphere, especially in cult titles or divine address.

### B. Contact through specialist cult networks
The borrowing could reflect shared priestly or sanctuary contact rather than broad language replacement.

### C. Elite-contact rather than mass bilingualism
If the administrative core remains non-Anatolian while ritual terminology shows Anatolian influence, that argues against “Linear A is just Anatolian” and more toward:
- a distinct Minoan language
- with Anatolian/Luwian contact layered into specific prestige domains

## 6. What this does NOT support
Current evidence does **not** support the stronger claim that:
- Linear A as a whole is simply Luwian or Anatolian
- the administrative language was broadly Anatolianized
- all attractive etymologies in the project belong in one Anatolian bucket

The contact zone appears selective, not total.

## 7. Best verifier conclusion
The strongest defensible conclusion from the current project materials is:

> Anatolian/Luwian contact in Linear A is real, but it appears to be concentrated in the religious and elite lexical register rather than spread evenly across the administrative core vocabulary. The best evidence remains `ja-sa-sa-ra-me`, with `du-pu₂-re` as an intriguing but less secure second case. This pattern favors prestige borrowing, cult contact, or elite interaction over wholesale language identity.

## 8. What would strengthen this further
A stronger version of this task would next require:
1. direct lexicon extraction of candidate Anatolian-looking forms from Younger's PDF using the now-installed `pdftotext`
2. comparison against known Luwian / Hittite / Lycian lexemes in a small candidate table
3. classification of each candidate by register:
   - ritual
   - elite/title
   - administrative
   - commodity
   - personal name

That would allow a more systematic test of the “ritual-only borrowing” hypothesis.
