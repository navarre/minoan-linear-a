# Site Restructuring Brief — April 2026

## Purpose
Translate current research and verifier findings into a clearer public-facing site structure that serves three different audiences without overclaiming.

## Core problem
The current site has real substance, but the front door mixes:
- public wonder
- corpus/data scale
- research conclusions
- sign catalog depth
- AI/research framing

This makes the site feel rich, but not well-sequenced for distinct users.

## Three audience lanes

### 1. Visitor / museum-goer / fascinated newcomer
Needs:
- wonder first
- place, objects, discovery history
- map, easy navigation, dramatic facts
- confidence that the site is serious

Should see first:
- What is Linear A?
- The oldest prayer in Europe
- Oldest nearby precursor / Arkhanes
- Knossos discovery history
- Most recent major find (Anetaki ivory scepter)
- Map of sites
- Ten findings

Should *not* see too early:
- sign taxonomy disputes
- morphology detail
- isolate/family arguments
- heavy corpus tooling

### 2. Docent / junior researcher / bridge user
Needs:
- public-safe accuracy
- confidence labels
- reusable explanations
- links from narrative to evidence

Should see:
- what is securely known
- what is probable
- what remains open
- site-by-site highlights
- selected corpus/gallery paths
- how to talk about the project without overclaiming

### 3. Researcher / specialist
Needs:
- provenance
- confidence discipline
- method clarity
- verifier/challenger framing
- ability to audit claims quickly

Should see:
- data sources and denominators
- verifier outputs
- AI methods framing
- analysis docs
- discrepancy / audit pages

## Recommended homepage structure

### Section order
1. **Hero / Why this matters**
   - what Linear A is
   - why it remains undeciphered
   - why Crete matters

2. **Three public hooks**
   - oldest precursor nearby
   - Knossos / discovery story
   - newest major find

3. **The prayer / what can be read**
   - carefully framed
   - confidence-labeled

4. **Map of sites**
   - place before data depth

5. **Choose your path**
   - Start Here
   - Explore the Corpus
   - Research & Methods

6. **Selected findings**
   - drawn from `ten-findings.md`
   - edited for confidence wording

7. **Research / method / AI / verifier layer**
   - lower on page, but easy to access

A visitor-first entry page:

### 2. Docent / Teaching Guide
Bridge page for junior researchers / museum staff:

### 3. Research & Methods
Should explicitly include:
- data sources

## Confidence framework to display publicly
Use the verifier's recommended confidence labels site-wide:
- **Data-verified**
- **PDF-verified / source-verified**
- **Source-reported**
- **Interpretive**
- **Speculative**

For public pages, these can be simplified visually to:
- Confirmed
- Strongly supported
- Open question
- Hypothesis

## AI framing recommendation
Avoid sounding mystical or promotional.

### Bad framing
- AI solved it
- AI can keep millions of connections in memory
- AI sees what humans cannot

### Better framing
- AI is used for corpus unification, large-scale cross-reference, consistency checking, hypothesis generation, and verifier/challenger review.
- Strong claims are tied to data artifacts, source checks, or explicit confidence labels.

### Suggested public wording
> This project uses frontier AI systems in two roles: research synthesis and verification/challenge. AI is not treated as an oracle. It is used to compare large bodies of scholarship and corpus data, surface patterns at scale, and stress-test claims against sources and extracted evidence.

## Pages most in need of revision

### `index.html`
Needs:
- better sequencing by audience
- fewer strong conclusions above the fold
- more confidence discipline
- clearer path split

### `ten-findings.md`
Strong asset, but should be revised for confidence wording before becoming a public flagship page.

### `signs.html`
Useful, but should be demoted from primary public navigation and nested under research/explore.

### `browse.html`
Strong tool for advanced users, not ideal as a first-stop visitor page.

### `sites.html`
Already one of the strongest public-facing assets; should be elevated in the visitor flow.

## Immediate implementation order
1. Create / revise **Start Here**
2. Rework homepage structure
3. Create **Docent / Teaching Guide**
4. Add **Research & Methods** page with AI + verifier framing
5. Move signs/audit deeper into research navigation

## Bottom line
The site should stop trying to greet all audiences in the same voice. The right structure is:
- wonder first for visitors
- confidence-labeled bridge layer for docents
- provenance/method clarity for researchers

That structure will make the research feel stronger, not weaker, because it will stop forcing every user through the same doorway.
