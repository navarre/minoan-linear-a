# The Mavrospelio Gold Ring (KN Zf 13)

## The Most Important Single Linear A Text

A gold signet ring found in 1926 by Arthur Evans in the Mavrospelio cemetery, east of the Palace of Knossos. Now in the Heraklion Archaeological Museum (HM 530). Dated MM III (~1700-1600 BCE).

19 signs engraved in a spiral (like the Phaistos Disc), reading from outer edge inward. No word dividers. All readings certain.

## This is NOT the Ivory Scepter

**KN Zf 13** (gold ring, 19 signs, 1926) is a **different object** from the **Anetaki ivory scepter** (~119 signs, recovered in 2017 excavations, presented at a 2021 conference, published 2025 by Kanta, Nakassis, Palaima & Perna). Both are from Knossos, both are exceptionally long for LA, both are prestige objects. But they are distinct.

The ivory scepter's full sign-by-sign transcription has NOT yet been published.

## Parsing the 19 Signs

### Proposed three-word parse:

```
a-re-ne-si-di-*301-pi-ke | pa-ja | ta-ri-se-te-ri-mu-a-ja-ku
     (8 signs)            (2 signs)        (9 signs)
```

**Word 1: a-re-ne-si-di-*301-pi-ke** — A complex name or divine epithet
- Sub-element **a-re-ne-si** is cross-referenced by Younger to A-RI-NI-TA
- **si-di** maps to the attested name SI-DI-JA
- Contains *301 at position 6 — the critical unknown

**Word 2: pa-ja** — A short name or epithet
- Attested independently at HT 41a.4, ARKH Zf 9
- Related to pa-ja-re (cf. Linear B pa-ja-ro, a personal name)

**Word 3: ta-ri-se-te-ri-mu-a-ja-ku** — The agglutinative segment
- Shows root-suffix-root-suffix pattern: TA-RI + SE + TE-RI + MU + A-JA-KU
- **TA-RI** and **TE-RI** both attested independently
- **-SE-** could be the instrumental/connective suffix
- **-MU-** could be a case/verbal marker
- **A-JA-KU** or **JA-KU** attested at MA 2b.2

## Why This Text Matters

1. **Complete and undamaged** — all 19 signs read with certainty
2. **Contains *301** mid-word — phonological context constrains its value
3. **Gold prestige object** — from a high-status burial, ritual/ceremonial significance
4. **Spiral format** — echoes Phaistos Disc, possibly a specific inscription genre
5. **Multiple known sub-sequences** — A-RE-NE-SI, SI-DI, PA-JA, TA-RI, TE-RI, JA-KU all appear elsewhere
6. **The 9-sign final segment** shows clear agglutinative suffix chaining — the strongest evidence of Minoan verbal/nominal morphology on a single artifact

## The Anetaki Ivory Scepter (separate object)

Published 2025: Kanta, A., Nakassis, D., Palaima, T.G., & Perna, M. "An archaeological and epigraphical overview of some inscriptions found in the Cult Center of the city of Knossos (Anetaki plot)." *Ariadne* Supplement Series 5, pp. 27-43.

- Ivory circle + handle, carved from elephant tusk
- ~119 signs across 12 metope panels (ring) + 4 handle sides
- Two different scribal hands
- Ring: logographic style resembling Cretan Hieroglyphic
- Handle: mixed script with vases, animals, numbers
- Content: ritual/ceremonial, recording offerings or sacrifices
- **Full transcription NOT YET published** — we cannot check for *301

When the full transcription is published, this will be the most important new data for Linear A studies since GORILA.
