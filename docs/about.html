About This Project

Who we are, what we're doing, and why

What This Is

This is an open-source computational research project aimed at making Linear A — the undeciphered writing system of Minoan Crete — accessible, searchable, and analyzable. We are building the most complete unified digital corpus of Linear A inscriptions, combining data from SigLA, lineara.xyz, GORILA (the foundational print corpus), and the RILA 2025 supplement.

The project includes a document gallery with inscription drawings, an interactive sign explorer, a site map with real geographic data, a glossary with confidence scores, and a growing body of original analysis — including morphological studies, hypothesis testing, and deep investigation of individual signs and inscriptions.

What This Is Not

This is not a decipherment claim. We have not cracked Linear A. Nobody has. What we have done is assemble the data, read the scholarship, run computational analyses, and document what can and cannot be concluded from the evidence. We are honest about uncertainty, and we assign confidence levels to every claim.

We believe the path to understanding Linear A runs through open data, reproducible methods, and honest engagement with the limits of what the evidence supports.

Methodology

The project follows these principles:

  • Confidence scores on everything. Every glossary entry, every sign value, every proposed reading carries an explicit confidence level: HIGH (arithmetically verified), MEDIUM (distributional evidence), LOW (single attestation or weak parallel), or SPECULATIVE.
  • Reproducibility. All data is in open JSON format. All analysis scripts are in Python, open source on GitHub. Results should be independently verifiable.
  • Source attribution. Every claim traces back to a specific published source or a specific computational analysis with documented methodology. We cite Salgarella, Younger, Melena, Steele, Schurr, Schoep, Bennet, Manning, and others — not because name-dropping looks good, but because the reader needs to evaluate our sources.
  • Honest about limits. When we say “the morphology is agglutinative,” we mean the suffix-chaining pattern is demonstrated in the data. When we say “the language is probably not Greek,” we mean the morphological evidence is difficult to reconcile with Greek — not that we have absolute proof.

Data Sources

  • GORILA (Godart & Olivier, 1976–1985) — the foundational 5-volume corpus of all Linear A inscriptions. Page images acquired; structured data being extracted.
  • RILA Supplément 1 (Del Freo & Zurbach, 2025) — 107 new documents. Index acquired; full concordances pending.
  • SigLA (Salgarella & Castellan, 2020–) — 772 documents with sign-level palaeographic data.
  • lineara.xyz (Hogan, based on Douros/GORILA) — 1,722 documents with Unicode transcriptions.
  • Younger’s Linear A Lexicon (2008–2024) — the most complete published word list.
  • 36 reference papers — read, summarized, and cross-referenced against our corpus data.

Collaboration

We actively seek:

  • Expert review. If you work on Aegean scripts and see errors, overclaiming, or missed evidence, we want to hear from you.
  • Data contributions. Museum inventory numbers, unpublished readings, photograph permissions — all welcome.
  • Technical collaboration. The computational side (distributional analysis, sign clustering, cross-script comparison) would benefit from specialists in NLP, computer vision, and historical linguistics.
  • The RILA 2025 full concordances. If you have access to the complete supplement, we would love to incorporate the 107 new documents.

All contributions are credited. All data remains open.

Where to See Linear A

The primary collection is at the Heraklion Archaeological Museum, Crete. Other museums with Linear A artifacts include the Chania Archaeological Museum, Siteia Archaeological Museum, Athens National Archaeological Museum, the Ashmolean Museum (Oxford), the British Museum (London), the Metropolitan Museum of Art (New York), and the Museo Archeologico Nazionale (Florence). A full museum guide is available in the project documentation.
