What is Linear A?

The Script

Linear A was the writing system of the Minoan civilization on Bronze Age Crete, used from approximately 1700–1450 BCE. Despite over a century of scholarship since Arthur Evans’s discoveries at Knossos, it remains undeciphered. The underlying language is unknown — it is not Greek, and its language family has never been conclusively identified.

The total known corpus comprises 1,534 inscriptions bearing 7,574 signs (per RILA 2025). This project has assembled 1,880 document records from multiple sources into a unified, structured, searchable corpus.

This Project

We are building the most complete computational resource for Linear A research. The corpus merges data from SigLA (772 documents with sign-level palaeographic data), lineara.xyz (1,722 documents from GORILA), and hand-curated ritual inscriptions.

Every document is tagged by source, object type, linguistic value, site, and period. The canonical data lives in three files: corpus.json, signs.json, and glossary.json.

Reproducibility over revelation. Confidence scores on everything.

The Corpus

Inscriptions survive on tablets (489), nodules (913), roundels (192), stone vessels (62), clay vessels (63), and other objects found across 55 sites in Crete and the Aegean.

Of the 1,880 documents, 554 have high linguistic value (tablets, stone vessels, lames) and drive most analysis. The remaining 1,037 are ethnographic — seal stamps and short marks that tell us about Minoan administrative practices but contribute little to linguistic analysis.

Key Findings

Confirmed: Natural Language

Word frequencies follow Zipf’s law (exponent 1.103, R²=0.901). Shannon entropy is 4.92 bits/syllable. This definitively rules out ciphers, codes, and purely symbolic systems.

The Libation Formula

a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja   ja-sa-sa-ra-me   i-da-ma-te
“[I] dedicate [this to] My Lady, Mother of Ida”

Found on 29+ stone vessels from 16 sites. The most important religious text in Linear A. Three-tier structure confirmed: core formula → deity epithet → deity name. 100% ritual-exclusive tokens.

52 Words Read

8 high confidence (arithmetic verification), 37 medium (distributional evidence), 5 low, 2 speculative. Includes administrative terms (ku-ro = total, ki-ro = deficit), place names (Phaistos, Kydonia, Sybrita, Siteia), and deity names. Full glossary.

Grammar Emerging

6–8 case suffixes identified: -ro (result/total), -te (dative/locative), -ja (adjectival/ethnic), -ra (agentive), -na (genitive), -me (possessive). Productive prefix a- dominates 8 of 10 largest word families. Word order: AGENT – COMMODITY – QUANTITY. No verb morphology detected in administrative texts.

Novel Discoveries

Full-corpus analysis (1,880 docs) revealed: near-zero vocabulary overlap between sites (Jaccard 0.000–0.016), 16% of common sign bigrams are forbidden (phonotactic constraints), sign A301 appears on 48 documents as both logogram and syllabogram, and figs (FIC) are the most commonly recorded commodity (49 docs, more than grain).

Unified Corpus

Corpus Browser

  • Sign explorer showing all attestations across the corpus

  • Interactive map of sites

Analysis Platform

In Progress

Statistical tools for the corpus. Frequency analysis, positional patterns, cross-site comparison, morphological detection. Open for other researchers.

  • 14 reproducible benchmarks with methodology and results
  • Zipf validation, entropy analysis, bigram/phonotactic analysis
  • Cross-site dialect variation analysis
  • Word class detection (admin terms vs. names vs. ritual)
  • Package tools for external use
  • Automated re-run against updated corpus data
4

Object Catalog

In Progress

Every physical object bearing Linear A, with metadata, images, provenance, and object-type tags. Not just linguistic content but archaeological context.

  • 1,880 documents with object type, site, period
  • 591 with scribe attribution, 1,062 with findspot
  • Linguistic value tagging (554 high, 289 medium, 1,037 low/ethnographic)
  • Link to GORILA page references for each document
  • Integrate tablet drawing images
5

Vocabulary & Grammar

Ongoing

Maintain a growing glossary with confidence scores and a grammar sketch. Evidence-based, falsifiable, honest about uncertainty.

  • 52-word glossary with confidence scores and evidence chains
  • 6–8 case suffixes identified with distributional evidence
  • Word boundary phonotactics mapped
  • Loanword directionality established
  • Expand glossary as corpus analysis deepens
  • Resolve sign *301

Inscription Sites

Linear A inscriptions have been found across Crete and the wider Aegean, from palace archives to mountain-top sanctuaries.

Haghia Triada

1,167 documents

South-central Crete. The richest source of Linear A, yielding 1,167 documents including tablets, nodules, and roundels. Primarily administrative/accounting documents recording commodities, personnel, and totals.

Zakros

53 documents

Eastern Crete palace. Administrative tablets and ritual inscriptions.

Khania

244 documents

Western Crete. Administrative records showing connections to other palace centers.

Phaistos

79 documents

South-central Crete palace. Administrative tablets from the neopalatial period.

Ioukhtas

16 documents

Peak sanctuary above Archanes. Stone libation tables with ritual formulas.

Syme

12 documents

Mountain sanctuary in eastern Crete. Libation vessels spanning several centuries.

Other Sites

309 documents across 49 sites

Knossos, Archanes, Malia, Petras, Psychro Cave, Palaikastro, Tylissos, and Aegean islands (Thera, Kea, Miletos).
