What is Linear A?

The Script

Linear A was used in Bronze Age Crete from approximately 1700–1450 BCE. It is the primary writing system of the Minoan civilization, predating the deciphered Linear B (Mycenaean Greek) by centuries.

Despite over a century of scholarship, Linear A remains undeciphered. The underlying language is unknown—it is not Greek, and its language family has never been conclusively identified.

Our Approach

This project applies computational methods to the corpus of ~1,400 known inscriptions. Rather than guessing translations, we build from the ground up:

  • Statistical frequency analysis
  • Positional pattern detection
  • Cross-site comparison
  • Hypothesis testing with confidence scores

Reproducibility over revelation. The math doesn’t lie.

The Corpus

Inscriptions survive on clay tablets, stone vessels, roundels, and other objects found across Crete and the Aegean. Two major categories:

  • Administrative: Accounting tablets recording commodities, quantities, and totals (mainly from Haghia Triada)
  • Religious: Libation formulas on stone vessels from peak sanctuaries and caves

Benchmark Results

Replication benchmarks test whether our computational tools can independently recover findings that scholars have already established.

Provisional Success Level 1

ku-ro as Summation Marker

Can we recover that ku-ro means "total" using only positional and numerical analysis?

74 occurrences as first token in quantity-bearing lines
5.3× more frequent than the next candidate (a-du: 14)

Example Lines

HT 1  ku-ro . GRA 33 HT 6  ku-ro . GRA 13 . OLE 1 . VIN 2 HT 17 ku-ro . FIC 60

ku-ro appears in a stable position before commodity + number expressions across dozens of tablets. The statistical signal is overwhelming.

Provisional Success Level 1

po-to-ku-ro as Grand Total

Does po-to-ku-ro function as an extended summation expression related to ku-ro?

2 occurrences in quantity-bearing lines
100% structural alignment with ku-ro pattern

Evidence

HT 93 po-to-ku-ro . GRA 576 HT 99 po-to-ku-ro . GRA 60

Both occurrences show identical structural behavior to ku-ro: line-initial position, followed by commodity + number. The larger quantities (576) suggest a "grand total" function. Sample size is small but structurally clear.

Inscription Sites

Linear A inscriptions have been found across Crete and the wider Aegean, from palace archives to mountain-top sanctuaries.

Haghia Triada

52 inscriptions

South-central Crete. The richest source of Linear A, yielding ~150 tablets (42 digitized). Primarily administrative/accounting documents recording commodities, personnel, and totals.

Zakros

14 inscriptions

Eastern Crete palace. Administrative tablets and ritual inscriptions.

Khania

12 inscriptions

Western Crete. Administrative records showing connections to other palace centers.

Phaistos

10 inscriptions

South-central Crete palace. Administrative tablets from the neopalatial period.

Ioukhtas

6 inscriptions

Peak sanctuary above Archanes. Stone libation tables with ritual formulas.

Syme Viannou

4 inscriptions

Mountain sanctuary in eastern Crete. Libation vessels spanning several centuries.

Other Sites

29 inscriptions

Knossos, Archanes, Malia, Petras, Psychro Cave, Palaikastro, Tylissos, and Aegean islands (Thera, Kea, Miletos).
